Badu’s breakthrough came in 1994, when she appeared as the opening act for R&B artist D’Angelo, marking a watershed moment in her career. It was at that performance that Kedar Massenburg, a well-known president and CEO of a record company known as “Kedar Entertainment,” became aware of Badu’s abilities and took note. Soon after, Badu was signed to his recording label, and her first studio album, “Baduizm,” was released the following year. Four songs were released from the album, which reached #2 on the Billboard charts upon its release, earning Badu critical praise for her unique musical approach.
